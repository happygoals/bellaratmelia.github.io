How can we help people to be mentally healthier?

How can we build an inclusive future, for mental health and well-being that satisfies all?

Motivation

Users with access to therapy session feel less likely to journal because session is already enough but sessions are expensive and college students have limited budget so an affordable journaling app for those students with limited free time can provide mental health care.

The major purpose of our project is to create a dynamic tool that allows college students to take care of their mental health on busy days with short but meaningful “happy thoughts”. With a simple process to keep the daily mood and gratitude logs on our app, students become aware of their wellbeing by taking action and gratitude for positive things/objects/events around them. The app also aims to encourage practicing gratitude and mindfulness practice for those who are suffering from anxiety and depression.

By writing and keeping the gratitude logs through our app, users will improve their mental health and change their perception of life. The app intends to teach the users how to be more thankful every day and treat themselves through the power of gratefulness.
